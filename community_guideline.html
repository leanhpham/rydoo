Introduction

Every day, people use GOPY to share their stories, observe the community through the eyes of others, connect with colleagues, make friends along with other social purposes. We're aware of the importance of GOPY in becoming a platform where everyone, specifically employees can communicate, raise opinions, share, search for information and request consulting relating to GOPY.

We take our role very seriously by keeping abuse off our service. That's why we have developed a set of Community Standards that outlines what is and is not allowed on GOPY. Our standards apply to all types of content worldwide. These are comprehensive standards by which, for example, content that is not viewed as hate speech still can be removed for violating our policy.

The goal of Community Standards is to create a safe environment which encourages everyone to express themselves . We've carefully crafted our policies based on information from communities and experts in areas like technology and public safety. Our policy also stems from the following principles:

Safety: Everyone needs to feel safe in order to build a strong community. We're committed to removing any content that promotes harmful real-world behavior, including (but not limited to) physical, financial, and emotional harm.

Voice: Our mission is to captivate diverse perspectives. We prioritize displaying content, even if some people disagree, unless removing it might prevent a particular hazard. Furthermore, we will allow content that may violate our standards if we believe the content is credible, meaningful, or important to the public interest. We only do this after carefully consider the public benefit of the content against the risk of real-world harm.

Fairness: Our communities are global and diverse and so are our policies. We've consistently and fairly applied them to a community spanning many regions, cultures, and languages. As a result, sometimes our Community Standards may not be as clear as we desire, leading to results contrary to our underlying purposes. For that reason, in some cases which we are given more context, decisions will be made based on the spirit of the policy rather than literal sense.

Everyone on GOPY plays an important role in keeping this platform safe and respectful. We require everyone to share, participate responsibly and inform us when seeing something that might violate our Community Standards. We make it easy for people to report potentially infringing content, including pages, groups, profile pages, personal content and / or comments for our review.

The consequences of violating our Community Standards will vary depending on the severity of the violation and the person's history on the platform. For example, we will give warning for first time violator, but if they continue to violate our policies, we may limit their ability to post to GOPY or disable their profile. We may also notify law enforcement when we believe there is a real danger of physical harm or a direct threat to the safety of the community.

Our community standards serve as a communication guide on GOPY. We will continue to develop these standards over time. In that spirit, we ask members of the GOPY community to adhere to these principles.

Section 1: Violence and Criminal Behavior

1. Violence and Incitement

Policy Rationale

It is our aim to prevent potential real-world hazards associated with content on GOPY. Although we understand that people often express disdain or disagreement by threatening or pretending to appeal to violence, we will remove language that incite or facilitate violent behavior. We remove content, disable accounts, and cooperate with law enforcement when we believe there is a genuine risk of physical harm or a direct threat to the safety of the community. We also try to consider language and context to distinguish unintentional statements from content that constitutes a potential threat to the safety of the public or private. To determine whether a threat is possible, we may also look at additional information such as public visibility and the risks to their physical safety.

In some cases, we see aspirational or conditional threats directed at terrorists and other violent actors (e.g. Terrorists deserve to be killed), and we deem those non credible absent specific evidence to the contrary.

Do not post:

Threats that could lead to death (and other forms of high-severity violence) targeting people or places where threat is defined as any of the following:

Statements of intent to commit high-severity violence. This includes content where a symbol represents the target and/or includes a visual of an armament or method to represent violence

Calls for high-severity violence including content where no target is specified but a symbol represents the target and/or includes a visual of an armament or method that represents violence

Statements advocating for high-severity violence; or a statement expressing the desire or conditions for the act of violence to be of a high severity

Content that requests or provides a service for hire to harm others ((e.g., hitmen, mercenaries, assassins) or advocates for the use of a hitman, mercenary or assassin against a target.

Admissions, statements of intent or advocacy, calls to action, or aspirational or conditional statements to kidnap a target

Threats that lead to serious injury (mid-severity violence) towards private individuals, unnamed specified persons, minor public figures, high risk persons, or high risk groups where threat is defined as any of the following:

Statements of intent to commit violence; or

Statements advocating violence; or

Calls for mid-severity violence including content where no target is specified but a symbol represents the target; or

Aspirational or conditional statements to commit violence; or

Content about other target(s) apart from private individuals, minor public figures, high risk persons, or high risk groups and any credible:

* Statements of intent to commit violence; or

* Calls for action of violence; or

* Statements advocating for violence; or

* Aspirational or conditional statements to commit violence

Section 2: Safety

1. Suicide and self-injury

Policy Rationale

In an effort to build a safe environment on GOPY, we will remove content that promotes suicide or self-injury, including some real-time descriptions and images that experts have warned against. We are able to induce others to perform the same behavior. Self-injury is defined as direct and intentional bodily injury, including self-amputation and eating disorder. We want GOPY to be a place where people can share experiences, raise awareness about these issues, and support each other. Therefore, we allow people to discuss suicide and self-injury.

We work with organizations around the world to assist people in distress. We also speak with suicide and self-injury experts to get information for our policies and practices. For example, experts have advised us not to take live videos of self-injuring behavior, as relatives and authorities will have the opportunity to help or provide resources.

Instead, we will remove any content that identifies and targets victims or survivors of self-injury or suicide, whether in a serious, humorous or fancy manner.

4. Bullying and Harrassment

Policy Rationale

Bullying and harassment happen in many places and come in many different forms, from making threats to releasing personally identifiable information, to sending threatening messages, and making unwanted malicious contact. We do not tolerate this kind of behavior because it prevents people from feeling safe and respected on GOPY.

We distinguish between public figures and private individuals because we want to allow discussion, which often includes critical commentary of people who are featured in the news or who have a large public audience. For public figures, we remove attacks that are severe as well as certain attacks where the public figure is directly tagged in the post or comment. For private individuals, our protection goes further: we remove content that's meant to degrade or shame, including, for example, claims about someone's sexual activity. We recognize that bullying and harassment can have more of an emotional impact on minors, which is why our policies provide heightened protection for users between the ages of 13 and 18.

Context and intent matter, and we allow people to post and share if it is clear that something was shared in order to condemn or draw attention to bullying and harassment. In certain instances, we require self-reporting because it helps us understand that the person targeted feels bullied or harassed. In addition to reporting such behavior and content, we encourage people to use tools available on GOPY to help protect against it.

We also have a Bullying Prevention Hub, which is a resource for teens, parents, and educators seeking support for issues related to bullying and other conflicts. It offers step-by-step guidance, including information on how to start important conversations about bullying.

Section 3: Objectionable Content

1. Hate speech

Policy Rationale

We don’t allow hate speech on GOPY because it creates an environment of intimidation and exclusion; and in some cases may promote offline violence.

We define hate speech as a direct attack against people on the basis of what we call protected characteristics: race, ethnicity, national origin, disability, religious affiliation, caste, sexual orientation, sex, gender identity and serious disease. We also protect refugees, migrants, immigrants and asylum seekers from the most severe attacks, though we do allow commentary and criticism of immigration policies. We define aggression as violent or degrading language, insults, calls for exclusion or isolation. We divide aggression into three levels of severity as described below.

We recognize that people sometimes share content that includes someone else’s hate speech to condemn it or raise awareness. In other cases, speech that might otherwise violate our standards can be used self-referentially or in an empowering way. At times, people show their anger when feelings are broken. On other occasions, they use gender-specific language to control membership in a health or active support group, such as a women-only breastfeeding group. Our policies are designed to allow room for these types of speech, but we require people to clearly indicate their intent. If intention is unclear, we may remove content.

Besides, people can write funny and social comments related to these topics. In addition, we believe that when using real identities, people are more responsible when sharing this type of comment.

Do not post:

Tier 1

Content targeting a person or group of people (including all subsets except those described as having carried out violent crimes or sexual offenses) on the basis of their aforementioned protected characteristic(s) or immigration status with:

Violent speech or support in written or visual form

Dehumanizing speech or imagery in the form of comparisons, generalizations, or unqualified behavioral statements (in written or visual form) to or about:

Insects

Designated dehumanizing comparisons, generalizations, or behavioral statements (in written or visual form)

Tier 2

3. Fake News

Policy Rationale

Reducing the spread of false news on GOPY is a responsibility that we take seriously. We also recognize that this is a challenging and sensitive issue. We want to help people stay informed without stifling productive public discourse. There is also a fine line between false news and satire or opinion. For these reasons, we don't remove false news from GOPY but instead, significantly reduce its distribution by showing it lower in the News Feed.

We are working to build a more informed community and reduce the spread of false news in a number of different ways, namely by

Disrupting economic incentives for people, Pages, and domains that propagate misinformation

Using various signals, including feedback from our community, to inform a machine learning model that predicts which stories may be false

Reducing the distribution of content rated as false by independent third-party fact-checkers

Empowering people to decide for themselves what to read, trust, and share by informing them with more context and promoting news literacy

Collaborating with academics and other organizations to help solve this challenging issue
